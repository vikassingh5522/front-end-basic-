Mountains / India
Kangchenjunga is the third-highest mountain in the world, standing at an elevation of 8,586 meters (28,169 feet). It lies on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, in the eastern Himalayas. Known for its majestic beauty and challenging terrain, Kangchenjunga is revered by locals as a sacred peak and is home to diverse ecosystems.
Nanda Devi** is the second-highest mountain in India, with an elevation of 7,816 meters (25,643 feet). It is located in the Garhwal Himalayas in Uttarakhand and is part of the Nanda Devi National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Revered in Indian mythology, the mountain is considered sacred and attracts adventurers and spiritual seekers alike.
Kamet is the second-highest peak in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand, India, standing at 7,756 meters (25,446 feet). It is part of the Zaskar range and is located near the Tibetan Plateau, making it somewhat remote and less climbed compared to other Himalayan peaks. Its name and striking appearance are steeped in both adventure and cultural significance.
Trisul is a group of three Himalayan peaks located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India, with the highest peak reaching an elevation of 7,120 meters (23,359 feet). Named after Lord Shiva's trident (Trishul) due to its three-pronged shape, it is a prominent part of Indian mythology.
**Rimo Kangri I** is the highest peak in the Rimo massif, located in the Karakoram range of Ladakh, India, with an elevation of 7,385 meters (24,229 feet). The Rimo group is part of the remote Siachen Glacier region, known for its rugged and pristine terrain. The name "Rimo" means "striped mountain," reflecting the distinct appearance of its ridges.
Hardeol, also known as the "Temple of God," is a majestic peak in the Kumaon Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, with an elevation of 7,151 meters (23,461 feet). It stands prominently in the northern wall of the Milam valley, near the Nanda Devi Sanctuary. Renowned for its steep slopes and challenging climbs, Hardeol is revered as a sacred mountain in local traditions.
Mamostong Kangri, also known as **K35**, is the highest peak in the Rimo Muztagh subrange of the Karakoram range in Ladakh, India, with an elevation of 7,516 meters (24,659 feet). It is the third-highest peak in India and is located near the Siachen Glacier. Known for its challenging climbs, it remains relatively unexplored due to its remote location and harsh conditions.
Anamudi is the highest peak in South India, standing at an elevation of 2,695 meters (8,841 feet). Located in the **Western Ghats** in Kerala, it is part of the Eravikulam National Park and the **Idukki District**. Anamudi is renowned for its rich biodiversity, including rare flora and fauna, and is a popular destination for trekking and nature enthusiasts.
